You need three things:

## The Big Three: Sun, Moon, Rising

Before you dive into the deep end, start here. These three placements make up roughly 80% of how you show up in the world.

### ‚òÄÔ∏è Your Sun Sign

**What it is:** The zodiac sign the Sun was in when you were born.

**What it means:** Your core identity. Your ego. The "you" that you're growing into throughout your life.

When someone asks "what's your sign?" this is what they mean. But here's the thing ‚Äî your Sun sign is just one piece. It's like saying your entire personality is "engineer" because that's your job title.

### üåô Your Moon Sign

**What it is:** The zodiac sign the Moon was in when you were born.

**What it means:** Your emotional inner world. How you feel. What you need to feel safe. The you that comes out at 2 AM or after three drinks.

Your Moon sign is often more accurate for how you actually *experience* life day-to-day. A Capricorn Sun with a Cancer Moon is going to feel very different from a Capricorn Sun with an Aries Moon ‚Äî even though they have the same "sign."

### ‚¨ÜÔ∏è Your Rising Sign (Ascendant)

**What it is:** The zodiac sign that was rising on the eastern horizon at the exact moment you were born.

**What it means:** Your social mask. First impressions. How you appear to others before they really know you.

This is why birth time matters so much. The Rising sign changes roughly every 2 hours, so being born at 6 AM vs 8 AM could give you a completely different vibe.

Mars in Aries is aggressive, direct, competitive ‚Äî it goes after what it wants headfirst.

Mars in Libra is diplomatic, partnership-oriented, sometimes passive-aggressive ‚Äî it goes after what it wants through relationships and charm.

The 12 houses are the *areas of life* where planetary energy plays out. Think of them as different rooms in a house ‚Äî each one has a purpose.

| House | Life Area | Keywords |

## Putting It Together

Here's a simple framework:

**[Planet] in [Sign] in [House]**

= **[What energy] expressed [how] in [what area of life]**

Example: **Moon in Scorpio in the 4th House**

= Emotional needs (Moon) expressed intensely and privately (Scorpio) around home and family (4th house)

Translation: This person probably has a deeply emotional, possibly complicated relationship with their family. Home is where they feel most intensely. They might be secretive about their personal life.

## Common Mistakes Beginners Make

**1. Obsessing over one placement**
Your chart is a whole system. Don't freak out because you have "Saturn in the 7th house." Context matters.

**2. Thinking signs = houses**
Aries ‚â† 1st house. They have similar themes, but they're not the same thing.

**3. Using the wrong house system**
Whole Sign and Placidus are the most common. Try both and see which resonates. (This is a whole debate in the astrology community.)

**4. Ignoring the Rising sign**
It literally determines your entire house structure. It matters. A lot.

**5. Reading horoscopes for your Sun sign only**
Read for your Rising sign too. Horoscopes are often written using Rising sign as the 1st house.

## What Now?

You don't need to memorize everything. Start with the Big Three:
1. Look up your Sun, Moon, and Rising signs
2. Read about each one
3. Notice how they interact

Then gradually add Mercury (how you think), Venus (how you love), and Mars (how you act).

The chart will start making sense. It's like learning a language ‚Äî overwhelming at first, then suddenly things click.
