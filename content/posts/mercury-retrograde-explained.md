## What "Retrograde" Actually Means

Mercury isn't actually moving backward. No planet ever does.

Retrograde is an optical illusion. It happens because Mercury orbits the Sun faster than Earth does. When Mercury "laps" us, it appears to move backward from our perspective â€” like when you're in a car passing another car and the slower car seems to move backward relative to you.

That's it. That's the astronomy.

### Communication Gets Crossed

Messages get misunderstood. Emails go to spam. You say one thing, people hear another. That text you thought was clear? Not so much.

**What to do:** Slow down. Re-read before you send. Assume miscommunication is possible and over-clarify.

### Technology Glitches

Computers crash. Files corrupt. Your phone does something inexplicable. Backups become important.

**What to do:** Back up your data before retrograde starts. Don't buy new electronics during this period if you can help it. Expect glitches and plan for them.

### Travel Goes Sideways

Delays, cancellations, lost luggage, wrong turns. Short trips are more affected than long ones.

**What to do:** Build in extra time. Double-check reservations. Have backup plans.

### Old Things Resurface

Exes text. Old friends reappear. That project you abandoned comes back up. Past issues demand resolution.

**What to do:** This is actually retrograde's gift. It's a chance to revisit, resolve, and close loops. Not everything that returns should stay â€” but some things deserve a second look.

### Contracts Get Complicated

Not the best time to sign major agreements, start new jobs, or launch new ventures. Not because they'll definitely fail, but because details get missed.

**What to do:** If you must sign, triple-check everything. If you can wait, wait.

## What Mercury Retrograde Is NOT

Let's kill some myths.

### It's Not an Excuse

"Sorry I'm late, Mercury is retrograde" is not a valid excuse. Retrograde might contribute to chaos, but your choices are still your choices.

### It's Not All Bad

Retrograde is great for:
- Revisiting old projects
- Reconnecting with people from your past
- Reviewing and revising work
- Reflecting on decisions
- Researching before committing

Notice a pattern? Everything starts with "re-." That's retrograde's energy â€” reviewing the past, not charging into the future.

### It's Not Three Weeks of Doom

Mercury goes retrograde 3-4 times a year, for about three weeks each time. If retrograde was truly catastrophic, we'd be in chaos for 25% of the year.

Life goes on during retrograde. People sign contracts, start jobs, travel, and communicate just fine. The energy just asks for more attention.

### It's Not the Only Transit That Matters

There's so much more happening in the sky than Mercury Retrograde. While everyone's panicking about Mercury, Saturn might be squaring your Moon and that's actually what's making your life hard.

Mercury Retrograde gets blamed for a lot of things that have nothing to do with Mercury.

## The Shadow Periods

Here's something most people don't know: retrograde has a warmup and cooldown.

**Pre-Shadow (1-2 weeks before):** Mercury slows down. Themes of the retrograde start emerging. You might notice early communication hiccups.

**Retrograde Proper (3 weeks):** The main event. The energy is strongest.

**Post-Shadow (1-2 weeks after):** Mercury speeds back up but is still covering territory it already crossed. Loose ends get tied up.

So while "retrograde" officially lasts three weeks, the full cycle is more like 6-8 weeks.
